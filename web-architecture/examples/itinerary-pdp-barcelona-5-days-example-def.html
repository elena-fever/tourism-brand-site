Queues, passes & realism

  • Hola Barcelona vs single T-casual: math it against Days 2–3 metro intensity—avoid buying twice mid-trip.
  • Sagrada + Güell same calendar? doable only with staggered entries—never share identical morning DNA (see Day 2 vs Day 3).
  • Cruise passenger surge: Gothic shoulders flood ~10:30–13:00—shift cathedral east earlier (Day 1 slot 1).
  • Luggage: hotel hold beats dragging wheels on Montserrat (Day 4)—confirm night-before.
  • Night buses: if pintxo crawl runs late, verify N-bus routes home—taxi may beat stale maps mentally.

FAQs — tied to the timeline

Weather, family pacing and compression questions reference concrete days above—not generic city lore.

It rains hard on Day 4 — what swaps?

Shelve Montserrat first—execute the museum rebound slot (Day 4 · 16:30) earlier with MNAC interior or push Museu Picasso from Day 1 if skipped. Keep dinner seated near a metro trunk to dry off.

Kids under 10 — which blocks flex?

Keep Day 1 waterfront and shorten Day 3 Montjuïc if meltdown risk; swap Day 4 Montserrat for MACBA + tactile workshops only if attention spans hate altitude trains.

I only have a weekend in Barcelona — how do I compress?

Run Day 1 evening strip + full Day 2 + sunrise half of Day 3 (Park Güell → brunch); drop Montserrat and Sitges outright. That yields a credible weekend in Barcelona plan without pretending five days fit.

Is this really a Barcelona 3 day itinerary too?

Days 1–3 are the canonical three days Barcelona schedule spine—dense but humane if you don’t smuggle extra museums sideways. Treat Days 4–5 as altitude/coast seasoning, not prerequisites.

Ultra-tight departure — where do I steal time?

Cut Day 5 Sitges entirely; move airport buffer earlier by collapsing Day 2 rooftop optional and trimming Day 3 Poble Sec crawl to one seated dinner—never steal time from Sagrada security padding.
